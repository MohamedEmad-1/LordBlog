I’m Mohamed Emad, a web developer and software engineer focused on building practical digital products that solve real business problems.

I’m the founder of [Evelx](https://evelx.com), where I’m building my own web presence, sharpening my engineering skills, and documenting the process through projects like this Astro blog.

What pulled me into computer science in the first place was simple: it felt infinite. Other fields seemed defined and bounded to me at the time, but software felt like an open world with no clear ceiling. That curiosity is still the main reason I build.

## What I do

I work across web development, technical operations, and systems problem-solving. My strongest value is not just writing code, but learning fast, connecting tools together, and getting things shipped.

I’ve worked with:

- Next.js, React, Astro, Vite, Tailwind CSS, and WordPress
- SQL databases, backend integrations, and form workflows
- Google Workspace administration and email migration
- ERP setup, IT support, networking, and systems administration
- Cloudflare, Hostinger, deployment pipelines, and production hosting

## My background

My computer science degree gave me the fundamentals: object-oriented programming, data structures, memory concepts, SQL, networking, systems thinking, and problem decomposition. I worked with C++, C#, Java, SQL, and some Python during that period.

What shaped me even more, though, was building outside the classroom. I learned quickly that I grow best by applying concepts to real projects, real users, and real constraints.

My path has not been perfectly linear. After graduating, I spent time in back-office customer support before moving back toward technical work. That period helped clarify something important: I do my best work when I’m building systems, improving workflows, and solving technical problems directly.

## Building Evelx

Evelx is a web development company I founded and lead. We're a small team building digital products for clients while continuously pushing our own standards. We work with businesses directly, collaborate with agencies, and take on projects where execution quality actually matters. The goal is straightforward: build great work, grow the team, and make Evelx the name people think of when they need something built right.

For the main [Evelx site](https://evelx.com), I used Next.js and treated it as a real production app. I worked on routing, image handling, deployment, database-connected forms, email notifications, GitHub-based deployment flow, and branch-based development. It also pushed me to get more comfortable with pull requests, release discipline, and maintaining a live site without breaking production.

Alongside that, I built this Evelx blog with Astro to have a faster, content-focused place for writing, sharing what I'm learning, and improving my engineering thinking across the full stack.
